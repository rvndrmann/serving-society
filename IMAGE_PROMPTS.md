# Serving Society — Image Prompts

AI image-generation prompts for each section of the website.
**Brand palette:** Purple `#5E2D6E` · Lime `#C6DA02` · White. Keep imagery warm, inclusive, authentic, and dignity-forward.

**Universal style suffix** (append to every prompt for consistency):
> *shot on 35mm, natural soft daylight, shallow depth of field, warm cinematic color grade, authentic candid expressions, no stock-photo look, high detail, 4k, photorealistic.*

---

## 1. Hero Section — Background Image / Banner

**Purpose:** A welcoming, uplifting banner behind or beside the hero headline.

**Prompt:**
> A diverse group photograph of an NDIS support worker smiling warmly alongside a person using a wheelchair in a sunlit Australian community park. Natural morning light, lens flare, soft bokeh of green trees in the background. The support worker wears casual professional attire; the participant looks confident and happy. Composition leaves negative space on the left for overlay text. Mood: empowering, dignified, optimistic. Rich purple and warm golden tones in the color grading.

**Alt prompt (abstract option):**
> A soft abstract gradient background in deep royal purple (#5E2D6E) transitioning to a lighter lavender, with gentle flowing organic shapes suggesting connection and care. Subtle lime-green accent strokes. Minimalist, modern, premium, no people.

## 2. About / "How We Support You" Section

**Purpose:** Support team in action, reinforcing person-centred care.

**Prompt:**
> Candid documentary photo of an Australian NDIS support worker sitting at a kitchen table next to an adult participant, reviewing a goals workbook together. Both are smiling, relaxed, making eye contact. Warm natural window light from the left, muted home interior, plants on the sill. Conveys trust, collaboration, person-centred planning. Include both hands on the table indicating partnership.

## 3. Mission / Vision / Values Cards (3 images)

### 3a. Mission
> Close-up of two hands — one younger, one older — clasped together in a gesture of support, sunlight filtering through. Shallow DOF, soft pastel background, warm tones. Represents compassion and commitment.

### 3b. Vision
> Wide sunny shot looking upward at a person with a prosthetic leg standing confidently on a grassy Australian hilltop, arms relaxed, smiling toward the horizon at sunrise. Open sky, lens flare. Represents aspiration, independence, possibility.

### 3c. Values
> Overhead flat-lay of a circle of diverse hands placed together on a wooden table — hands of different ages, skin tones, abilities, including one with an assistive device. Soft natural light. Represents inclusivity, respect, collaboration.

## 5. Services Grid (6 images)

**Style note:** Either (A) warm documentary photography OR (B) consistent flat illustrations. Pick one direction for the whole grid.

### 5a. In-Home Support
> Candid photo of a support worker gently helping an older adult participant prepare a cup of tea in a bright Australian home kitchen. Warm morning light, clean modern interior, both relaxed and smiling.

### 5b. Community Access
> A support worker and a young adult participant laughing while exploring a local farmers' market, reusable tote bags, colorful produce stalls in the background. Bright daylight, authentic street photography feel.

### 5c. Capacity Building
> A participant practicing independent living skills — budgeting at a desk with laptop, calendar, and notebook — while a coach points helpfully at the screen. Warm home-office light, encouraging atmosphere.

### 5d. Therapeutic Supports
> An allied health professional (occupational therapist) guiding an adult participant through a hand-coordination exercise with therapy putty in a bright clinic room. Professional yet warm, clinical equipment softly blurred.

### 5e. Restrictive Practices
> A respectful meeting scene — three professionals reviewing a behaviour support plan document at a conference table. Focus on the paperwork and engaged discussion rather than faces. Calm, clinical, professional atmosphere. (Avoid any imagery of restraint.)

### 5f. SDA (Specialist Disability Accommodation)
> Exterior architectural photo of a modern accessible Australian home with a gentle ramp, wide doorway, landscaped garden path, and solar panels. Bright sunny afternoon, blue sky, lime-green hedging. Clean contemporary design.

## Representation & Sensitivity Checklist

When generating or sourcing images, ensure:

- [ ] Diverse representation across age, ethnicity, gender, and disability
- [ ] People with disability shown as active, capable, and central (not passive recipients)
- [ ] Support workers shown as peers and partners, not authority figures
- [ ] No medicalised, institutional, or pitying imagery
- [ ] Australian context where possible (landscapes, housing, signage)
- [ ] Visible consent cues — relaxed body language, genuine smiles, eye contact
- [ ] Include assistive technology naturally (wheelchairs, canes, hearing aids, AAC devices) without making them the focal point
